About

What is SADJ?

SADJ is a collection of Computer Sciencestudents at Saint Martin's University comprised of Spencer Phillips, Alec Williams, Draevin Luke, and James Hale. We have worked as a group to complete projects in a variety of classes, and came together to complete this project for CSC250 - Web Programming and Management.

What good is an Albertsons Monopoly Simulation?

For those that have played the Albertsons Monopoly game before, this website will come as a great relief. In the original game, players collect small pieces which are then affixed to a paper gameboard. These pieces are easily lost, often fall off, and are a hassle to affix in the first place. Using this tool, users can keep the pieces in an envelope or other container and index their inventory digitally. Upon finding that they have a prize, they can then submit the pieces as required by the terms and conditions of the game.

Please note that this website does not offer any support for nor take any support from the Albertsons Monopoly game, and can offer no assistance with submission of pieces for prizes. This simulation of the gameboard is offered merely as a matter of convenience to avoid frustrations with the physical gameboard and pieces.

How did you build such a sick website?

This website is built on the Propeller framework using our experience with HTML, CSS, JS, PHP, and SQL gained through our coursework at Saint Martin's. Using elements from Bootstrap and the Material Design guidelines from Google, we were able to create a visually appealing and consistent site with features to meet the requirements of both the project and our prospective users.
